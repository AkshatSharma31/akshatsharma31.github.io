### Project Overview
This independent theoretical project focuses on the electronic-structure features and gas-phase stabilities of complex Zintl cluster frameworks. By modeling electron-counting paradigms and orbital interactions, this work evaluates the thermodynamic drivers that stabilize highly charged cages.

### Technical Approach & Findings
* **Electronic Structure Modeling:** Performed comprehensive geometry optimizations using ORCA 6.0 at the B3LYP-D4/def2-SVP level of theory.
* **Heteroatomic Stability:** Analyzed the electronic stability of the heteroatomic Indium-Antimony cage [In₄Sb₅]³⁻. Calculations confirmed a substantial HOMO-LUMO gap of 2.71 eV in the gas phase, indicating a robust intrinsic kinetic stability that satisfies the 40-electron 'Nido' framework without requiring immediate crystal lattice stabilization.
* **Solvation Effects:** Contrasted the chemical robustness of [In₄Sb₅]³⁻ against the homoatomic [Ge₉]⁴⁻ cluster, demonstrating via a Conductor-like Polarizable Continuum Model (CPCM in Ethylenediamine) how high-charge homoatomic cages rely heavily on dielectric screening to avoid electron detachment.
