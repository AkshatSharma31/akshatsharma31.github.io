### Project Overview
[cite_start]A fundamental physical chemistry assay tracking how ambient environmental variables influence the crystallization energy landscape and polymorphic stability profiles of Paracetamol and Ibuprofen, conducted under the supervision of Prof. Soumyajit Roy[cite: 205, 320, 321].

### Key Milestones
* [cite_start]**Metastable Phase Isolation:** Synthesized the elusive and metastable Form II (orthorhombic) of Paracetamol from Form 1 (monoclinic)[cite: 209, 213, 216]. [cite_start]The phase transition was achieved by melting Form 1 at 169°C and allowing it to recrystallize, utilizing ethanol as a solvent to yield needle-like structures[cite: 211, 212, 213, 216].
* [cite_start]**Powder-XRD Analytics:** Evaluated the crystal structures using Powder-XRD data, mapping intensity against 2-theta angles to assign h, k, l values and calculate planar spacing[cite: 208, 301, 302].
* [cite_start]**Colloidal Solution Profiling:** Analyzed Ibuprofen crystallization by preparing colloidal solutions in varying concentrations of water and DMSO at different temperatures[cite: 288, 289, 296, 297].
