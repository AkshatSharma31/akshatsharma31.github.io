### Project Overview
This project was executed during a research internship at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Ropar under the supervision of Prof. C.M. Nagaraja. The research pipeline focused on the synthesis of N-Heterocyclic Carbene-Based Porous Organic Polymers (NHC-POP) and evaluating their efficacy as active materials for atmospheric carbon dioxide ($CO_2$) capture and catalytic conversion.

### Core Technical & Synthetic Training
During this intensive period, I was formally trained in advanced organic synthesis protocols, reaction monitoring, and material isolation, specifically handling sterically demanding carbene precursors.

* **Inert-Atmosphere Synthesis:** Assisted in the preparation and setup of complex polymeric reactions, mastering gas purging techniques to maintain strict inert atmospheres required for sensitive NHC chemistry. 
* **Advanced Isolation & Purification:** Executed rigorous post-synthetic workups to isolate pure polymer fractions. This included continuous liquid-liquid phase separations, solvent removal via rotary evaporation, high-speed centrifugation, and targeted vacuum drying procedures.
* **Analytical Profiling:** Trained in the interpretation of Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) spectroscopy to track intermediate conversions and validate the structural integrity of the synthesized N-heterocyclic carbene ligands. 
* **Reaction Engineering:** Gained hands-on bench experience with thermal reflux systems, condensers, and precision chemical monitoring, mapping the reaction pathways and structural representations utilizing ChemDraw.

### Environmental Application
The ultimate objective of the synthetic work was to leverage the localized high-electron density of the NHC active sites within the porous organic matrix. This structural motif serves as a highly efficient, heterogeneous thermodynamic trap to capture and catalytically convert $CO_2$ into value-added chemical feedstocks, addressing critical challenges in green energy and emission controls.
